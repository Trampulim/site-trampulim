The Group

Our Story

Thirty years of research, creation and stage — from Belo Horizonte to Brazil and the world.

Early steps (1994–2000)

Our story begins in 1994, and in 1998, with the creation of Spasso – Popular Circus School, we gained a space to house our activities. In those early days we staged O Circo, O Filme, Panis Et Circencis, O Cortejo em Busca do Riso (with Grupo Armatrux), O Bolo and Oraprocircus. In 2000, we left Spasso and moved our activities to Gonguê – Cultura, Tradição e Arte, a space founded by Lênis Rino, which also became our home.

The Clown Game (2001–2005)

The following year, 2001, we staged A Ponte, and in 2002 we presented the scenic-musical show Álbum de Família. The show Uma Surpresa para Benedita, premiered in 2003, marks the start of our clown research, which we named The Clown Game and later, in 2023, renamed PalhaçaRIA perCURSO AFETAdo. Two years on, we were already touring stages in Brazil and Canada, an extensive tour that culminated in studies with Canadian master teacher Sue Morrison. That experience was a watershed, leading us to also delve into improvisation.

The Invasion and music (2006–2010)

In 2006, the concept of INVASION emerges, embodied in the intervention Invasão de Palhaços. This idea of occupying spaces with art and joy spread across many projects, such as Riso Passageiro, which invaded buses and metros in five Brazilian capitals, and the festival ÍMPETO – World Invasion of Clowns and Everyone Else, held in 2007, 2010 and 2022.

Our research expanded into music with the show Pratubatê (2008), which uses the power of drums to turn the audience into the protagonist. That same year, we premiered Manotas Musicais, exploring music as a playful tool for the clown. When Gonguê closed in 2008, we founded a new home in the Jardim América neighbourhood: ET – Espaço Trampulim, where we developed several projects and productions until 2013.

National and international touring (2011–2017)

In 2011, we created Experimento, an innovative show with brand-new scenes at every performance. In 2014, we strengthened ties with Portugal through workshops and exchanges. Two years later, we joined SESC’s Palco Giratório, bringing our work to 37 Brazilian cities. In 2017, we premiered Acorda, with a new aesthetic aimed at adult audiences.

New stages (2018–2024)

In the following years, we intensified the touring of shows and workshops across Brazil, carried out research on clown and improvisation and, in 2020 and 2021, dived into audiovisual production. In 2022, we premiered the improv show Trampulimprovisa and the solo Partida, awarded in Belo Horizonte. In 2023, we flew to Portugal for an artistic residency and premiered Casamento, directed by Mexican José Luís Saldanha. The following year, 2024, we premiered A Rainha Procura, directed by César Gouvêa, and presented Partida at the Tem Graça Festival in Lisbon.

Partnerships that shape us

Throughout our journey, we have stood out for our partnerships with many directors, such as Geraldim Miranda, Gabriel Guimard, Rodrigo Matheus, Tiago Gamboggi, Ana Virgínia Guimarães, Sérgio Pena, Rogério Sette Câmara, Fernando Escrish, Paula Manata, José Luis Saldaña and César Gouvêa. This constant collaboration drives the group’s creativity and renewal.
